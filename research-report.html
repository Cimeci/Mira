I — Competitive Landscape

II — Why They All Fail

Five structural failures no existing tool has solved

A. The hash dependency problem

Every major tool requires the victim to possess the original image to generate a hash. Deepfakes are fabricated — victims have nothing to hash. A minor crop or re-encoding changes the hash, and abusers can generate 100 perceptually-distinct variations in 30 seconds.

B. Fragmented, voluntary platform coverage

StopNCII partners with ~15 platforms out of hundreds of relevant hosts. Telegram — the primary distribution channel for deepfake NCII at scale — is completely outside the system. Dark web and re-hosting sites regenerate under new domains within hours.

C. Catastrophic victim burden

No tool proactively monitors for a victim's likeness. Every system requires victim initiation, but victims often don't know content exists until it has already spread. The TAKE IT DOWN Act requires a separate written notice to each platform, per URL, while the victim is in acute distress.

D. Geographic and legal patchwork

TAKE IT DOWN Act is US-only, effective May 2026. Pre-2025 state laws excluded AI-generated content. Non-US hosting platforms ignore US law. There is no global equivalent.

E. Speed mismatch

Deepfake content spreads to tens of thousands in minutes. Human review and platform removal cycles operate in hours to days. The 48-hour window is aspirational; no automated compliance monitoring exists.

III — Opportunity Space

Eight gaps an agentic workflow could fill

No existing system combines proactive monitoring, content-level detection, automated notice filing, and compliance tracking into a single victim-facing pipeline. These are the specific gaps we can address.

  • 01
    Proactive monitoring without the original image Face-embedding + reverse image search on a recurring schedule across known distribution channels — no hash of an original needed.
  • 02
    Content-level deepfake detection AI classifiers for GAN/diffusion artifacts that work on never-before-seen content, not just hash matches.
  • 03
    Automated multi-platform notice filing Parallel TAKE IT DOWN Act + DMCA notices to all relevant platforms from a single victim input, properly formatted per each platform's requirements.
  • 04
    Forensic evidence collection Timestamped screenshots, metadata capture, cryptographic signing for legal admissibility — automated, not manual.
  • 05
    Re-hosting monitoring loop Persistent agent that detects re-uploads after takedown and automatically re-files — the most common failure mode today.
  • 06
    Platform compliance tracking + FTC escalation Verify 48-hour removal window; auto-generate FTC complaints when platforms miss the deadline.
  • 07
    Multilingual intake LLM-powered triage in any language with jurisdiction-specific legal guidance. No equivalent exists today.
  • 08
    Unified case dashboard Persistent state across all platforms, URLs, removal statuses, and legal filings — one place instead of dozens of browser tabs.

IV — Technology Assessment

What Gemini Computer Use can actually do

As of June 2026, computer use is a native capability in gemini-3.5-flash alongside function calling and Search grounding. The model takes screenshots of a live browser, reasons about what it sees, and emits concrete UI actions — clicks, keyboard input, scrolling. It is not DOM-scraping; it operates visually.

Feasibility by workflow step

Workflow Step Verdict Notes
Navigate social media platforms, find misused images Moderate Works technically; human needed to confirm victim identity
Navigate scam/forum sites to document misuse High Standard web navigation; no special restrictions
Navigate adult/porn platforms Blocked Safety filters refuse to process explicit screenshots — requires human-in-loop
Fill platform reporting forms (Instagram, X, Reddit…) High Well-scoped structured task — Gemini's core strength. Up to 10 parallel sessions.
Submit DMCA takedown requests High Form-filling with known fields; strong use case
File TAKE IT DOWN Act notices High Same as DMCA; structured form with defined legal fields
Handle CAPTCHAs Blocked Policy-prohibited; requires human confirmation step at each CAPTCHA
Auto-accept Terms of Service Blocked Policy-prohibited; agent pauses, human confirms
Submit to foreign authorities with web portals Moderate Technically feasible per jurisdiction; requires per-country workflow config
Fully autonomous end-to-end (no human) Low CAPTCHA breaks + ToS breaks + adult content breaks require human steps

Gemini Computer Use is the right tool for the form-filling and portal-submission layer. It cannot be the whole pipeline.

The adult content site navigation problem is not purely a safety-filter issue — it is a policy and legal liability problem for Google. The right answer is a human-in-the-loop hybrid: human confirms which URLs to act on, agent automates every submission from that point forward.

V — What We Build

Proposed architecture

01

Victim Intake

Upload reference photo(s) → face embedding generated client-side. No image stored on server. Victim provides name, country, consent.

Next.js Google Vision API
02

Discovery Layer — Automated

Reverse image search APIs + Gemini Computer Use navigates social media, forums, image hosting sites. Returns list of URLs with screenshots as evidence.

Automated Gemini Computer Use Playwright/CDP
03

Human Confirmation — Required

Victim reviews flagged URLs, confirms which to act on. This is also where adult site content gets human review — bypasses the safety filter problem and is the right UX: victims should approve actions taken in their name.

Human in loop ~30 seconds
04

Agentic Reporting Layer — Automated

Gemini Computer Use drives parallel sessions: platform takedowns (Instagram/X/Reddit/TikTok), DMCA notices, TAKE IT DOWN Act notices, country-specific authority forms. Evidence package generated with cryptographic hash.

Tracking & Compliance Loop

48h compliance monitoring per URL, auto-re-file on re-hosting, FTC escalation when platforms miss deadline. Unified case dashboard with status per platform.

VI — Competitive Position

Why this is different from Protect Privacy AI

Protect Privacy AI (launched May 2026) is the closest existing analog. It is US-only, focused on TAKE IT DOWN Act compliance, and requires victims to already know where their content is. Here is the gap:

Dimension Protect Privacy AI Mira
Content detection Victim must find URLs themselves Proactive monitoring via face embeddings — no hash of original needed
Deepfake support Unknown Face-based detection works on fabricated content
Geographic scope US only (TAKE IT DOWN Act) Multi-country authority mapping
Agentic automation Unknown mechanism Gemini Computer Use driving form submissions in parallel
Re-hosting monitoring Not documented Persistent agent loop re-files automatically
Adult site coverage Unknown Human-in-loop hybrid — honest about the limit, right UX anyway

VII — Hackathon Verdict

The 3-minute jury demo path

Demo score is 50% of total. Everything below is chosen because it is observable in real time by a jury, not because it's the most technically interesting part.

1

Victim uploads a reference photo

Face embedding generated in the browser. Victim confirms: "Search for misuse of my likeness." Simple, human-readable intake form — nothing that looks like a dev tool.

2

Agent discovers a matching URL and collects evidence

Gemini Computer Use navigates a pre-seeded demo target (reliable, no live adult content). Screenshot captured, timestamped, hash signed. Victim reviews and approves the takedown batch — one click.

3

Live form submission — screen visible to jury

The agent opens a real reporting form, navigates fields, submits. Jury watches the browser controlled by the agent in real time. Dashboard updates: case open, submission logged, 48h countdown started.

What to cut for the hackathon

Feature Decision Reason
Adult site navigation Human-in-loop Known policy blocker — frame as a deliberate UX choice, not a gap
Real-time re-hosting monitor Fake it Demo with a pre-staged re-upload; real loop takes hours to show
Full country authority mapping Scope to 2–3 Show France, US, UK — enough to prove the concept
FTC escalation flow Dashboard only Show the timer and the "escalate" button; don't wire the actual FTC form for demo
Deepfake classifier Skip Too much time to train; say "pluggable" in the pitch — jury will believe it
